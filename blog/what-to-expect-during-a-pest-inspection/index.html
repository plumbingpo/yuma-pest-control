What Happens During a Pest Inspection in Yuma, AZ

Home / Blog / What Happens During a Pest Inspection in Yuma, AZ

Whether you are buying a home, dealing with a suspected infestation, or simply want peace of mind, a professional pest inspection is one of the smartest steps you can take to protect your property. But if you have never scheduled one, you may not know what actually happens during the visit. This guide from Yuma Pest Control walks Yuma, AZ homeowners through exactly what to expect, from the first knock to the final recommendations.

Before the Inspection

A thorough inspection goes smoothly with a little preparation. You will want to provide clear access to areas the technician needs to examine, including the attic, garage, basement or crawl space, and the perimeter of the home. Moving stored items away from walls and clearing space under sinks and around appliances helps the technician inspect effectively. It is also helpful to jot down any signs you have noticed, such as droppings, sounds in the walls, damaged wood, or live pests, and where you saw them. The more information you share, the more targeted the inspection can be.

The Exterior Inspection

Most inspections begin outside, because that is where pests enter and where many problems originate. The technician examines the foundation, siding, and roofline for cracks, gaps, and other entry points. They check for mud tubes that indicate termites, wasp and bee nests under eaves, rodent burrows and runways, and conditions that attract pests such as standing water, wood-to-soil contact, and overgrown vegetation against the home. This exterior survey often reveals the root causes of interior pest activity.

The Interior Inspection

Inside, the technician systematically works through the home, paying special attention to the areas pests favor. They inspect kitchens and bathrooms for moisture and food sources, check baseboards and corners for droppings and trails, and examine cabinets, closets, and storage areas for signs of activity. Attics, basements, and crawl spaces receive close attention because these hidden spaces are where rodents nest, termites tunnel, and many infestations go undetected. Using a flashlight, moisture readings, and trained observation, the technician looks for evidence most homeowners would miss.

What the Technician Is Looking For

Throughout the inspection, an experienced technician searches for both active pests and the conditions that invite them. Key findings include:

  • Evidence of pests — droppings, shed skins, wings, egg cases, and gnaw marks.
  • Damage — hollow-sounding wood, chewed wiring, or compromised structural materials.
  • Entry points — gaps, cracks, and openings that allow pests inside.
  • Conducive conditions — moisture, clutter, food sources, and harborage areas.
  • Pest identification — confirming exactly which species is present, since treatment depends on it.

After the Inspection: Your Custom Plan

Once the inspection is complete, the technician reviews the findings with you in plain language. You will learn what pests, if any, were found, the extent of any activity, the conditions contributing to the problem, and the recommended next steps. If treatment is warranted, you receive a custom plan with transparent, upfront pricing and no hidden fees. A good inspection leaves you informed and in control, never pressured.
